GST Calculator: Quickly Find Your Tax Amount

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is something we all deal with—whether we’re buying a product, paying for a service, or running a business. But calculating GST manually can be confusing. That’s where a GST Calculator comes in!

This simple tool helps you quickly find out how much GST you need to pay or charge, saving time and avoiding errors.

How Does GST Work?

GST is a value-added tax applied to goods and services in India. It has different tax slabs:

  • ✔ 5% – Essential goods (groceries, medicines, etc.)
  • ✔ 12% & 18% – Standard tax rates for most products and services
  • ✔ 28% – Luxury items (cars, high-end appliances, etc.)

How to Calculate GST?

There are two main cases:

1️⃣ Adding GST to a Price

If you want to find the final price after adding GST, use this formula:

Final Price = Original Price + (Original Price × GST% / 100)

2️⃣ Removing GST from a Price

If you already have a GST-included price and want to find the original amount:

Original Price = GST-Inclusive Price / (1 + (GST% / 100))

Example: See It in Action

Let’s say you’re buying a product worth ₹1,000 with 18% GST.

GST Amount = 1,000 × 18 / 100 = ₹180

Final Price = 1,000 + 180 = ₹1,180

If ₹1,180 is the GST-included price and you want to find the original amount:

Original Price = 1,180 / 1.18 = ₹1,000

GST Calculator FAQs

GST is a value-added tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India. It replaced multiple indirect taxes like VAT, service tax, and excise duty, simplifying the tax structure and creating a unified market.
The GST Calculator estimates the GST amount and the total price of a product or service based on the following inputs:
  • Net Price: The price of the product or service before GST.
  • GST Rate: The applicable GST rate (e.g., 5%, 12%, 18%, or 28%).
It calculates the GST amount and the total price using the formula:
GST Amount = (Net Price × GST Rate) / 100
Total Price = Net Price + GST Amount
You need to provide:
  • Net Price: The price of the product or service before GST.
  • GST Rate: The applicable GST rate (e.g., 5%, 12%, 18%, or 28%).
The GST rates in India are divided into four slabs:
  • 5%: Essential items like packaged food, medicines, and transport services.
  • 12%: Processed foods, computers, and business-class air tickets.
  • 18%: Most goods and services, including electronics, restaurants, and financial services.
  • 28%: Luxury items like cars, tobacco, and aerated drinks.
